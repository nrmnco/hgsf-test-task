# Essential Knots

Five knots will cover most practical situations:

1. **Bowline** — forms a fixed loop at the end of a rope. Does not slip under load but also doesn't jam, so it can be untied after heavy loading.
2. **Clove hitch** — quick attachment of a rope to a post or rail. Easy but not load-secure on its own; add a couple of half hitches for a permanent tie-off.
3. **Figure-eight (on a bight)** — used in climbing to tie into a harness. Strong, easily inspected, and resistant to jamming compared to some alternatives.
4. **Taut-line hitch** — an adjustable loop often used on tent guy lines.
5. **Double fisherman's** — joins two ropes of similar diameter; common for prusik loops in climbing.

A good habit: learn the failure modes of a knot before relying on it, and always dress and set a knot before loading it.
